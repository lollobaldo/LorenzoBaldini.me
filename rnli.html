External Bleeding

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pale, Cold, Response V, P, U

Keep injured casualties warm and handle gently

EXTERNAL BLEED

Sit or lie casualty down
Expose and examine wound

Apply direct pressure to site and elevate if possible

Apply trauma dressing

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

If dressing bleeds through SLOWLY use windlass technique

If bleeding not controlled, treat as Life Threatening Bleed

If casualty goes unconscious maintain airway CARD 1

LIFE THREATENING BLEED

Apply immediate direct or indirect pressure

For limb bleeds use torniquet

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

DO NOT release torniquet

Note torniquet application time on cards / torniquet / head

Attempt to get casualty to hospital in under 2 hours

If casualty goes uncontious maintain airway CARD 1

NOTES

Do not pull out embedded objects. Pad around objects or open fractures. Pack deep holes with dressings, then apply pressure.
If limb is crushed for more than 15 minutes and evacuation is necessary, apply torniquet just prior to release

Internal Bleeding

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pale, Cold, Response V, P, U, Bruising, Swelling, Hardening in the abdomen

Keep injured casualties warm and handle gently

Exhamine 4 possible locations:
CHEST / ABDOMEN / PELVIS OR LIMB

Lie casualty HEAD DOWN
Raise feet if possible

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Insulate and keep warm

Monitor and rapidly evacuate SLIGHTLY HEAD DOWN

NOTES

Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Fractures

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pain, Pale, Swelling

Keep injured casualties warm and handle gently

CLOSED FRACTURES

Remove watches / jewellery from affected limb

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Immobilise fracture using frac straps and splints.
For lower limbs use 6 frac straps including a pelvic strap

Insulate and reassure
Evacuate in stretcher of choice

OPEN FRACTURES

Control life treathening bleeding, if present

Remove watches / jewellery from affected limb

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Apply dressing to wound, pack around bone ends but DO NOT push back in

Immobilise fracture using frac straps and splints.
For lower limbs use 6 frac straps including a pelvic strap

Insulate and reassure
Evacuate in stretcher of choice

NOTES

Transport HEAD DOWN if unconscious
Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Chest Injuries

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pain, Pale, Deformity, Wounds

NEVER GIVE ENTONOX

Keep injured casualties warm and handle gently

PENETRATING INJURY

If BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Pad around embedded objects
DO NOT PULL OUT

Seal any holes in chest with trauma dressing
BUT BREAK SEAL IF CASUALTY WORSENS

If conscious transport casualty in a sitting position leaning towards the injured side

If unconscious lie casualty on their side, injured side down UNLESS embedded object

Apply dressing to any additional wounds and apply pressure

Monitor closely and evacuate in stretcher of choice

BLUNT IMPACT

If BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

If conscious transport casualty in a sitting position leaning towards the injured side

If unconscious lie casualty on their side, injured side down

Monitor closely and evacuate in stretcher of choice

If unconscious TRANSPORT SLIGHTLY HEAD DOWN

NOTES

Wound size does not equal severity
Remove chest seal when using BVM
Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Facial Burns

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pain, Pale, Burnt hair

If chemical burn, flush with water and seek medical advice via radio

Suspect airway swelling

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If no entonox available and BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Cool facial burns for at least 10 minutes

If wheezing give 2 puffs SALBUTAMOL INHALER via spacer

Dress facial burns if possible and if necessary cool for a further 10 minutes

Monitor and if required follow by 2 puffs every 2 minutes to a maximum of 10 puffs in 10 minutes

Rapidly evacuate, avoid lying conscious casualty down

NOTES

Transport HEAD DOWN if unconscious
DO NOT burst blisters, or apply adhesive dressing
DO NOT remove clothing stuck to a burn
Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Body Burns

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Pain, Pale, blistering, Charring

If chemical burn, flush with water and seek medical advice via radio

Remove from cause
Remove watches / jewellery from affected area

Cool burn for at least 10 minutes

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours

If BIG SICK and no entonox available GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Dress burns with cling film or burn bags and if necessary cool for a further 10 minutes

Monitor and rapidly evacuate

NOTES

Transport HEAD DOWN if unconscious
DO NOT burst blisters, or apply adhesive dressing
DO NOT remove clothing stuck to a burn
Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Spinal injuries - Lying

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Numbness, Pain, Pins and needles, Incontinence, Loss of movement

Reassure, approach from the front, ask not to move

Hold head and neck in neutral alignment

Watch for vomiting and clear airway as required

If in pain - GIVE ENTONOX UNLESS the casualty has head or chest injuries or has been diving in the last 24 hours BUT be aware of masking pain

If BIG SICK and no entonox available GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Consider if you have to move BIG SICK / DANGER
or can you stabilise in location LITTLE SICK

If moving casualty, size and apply collar as long as airway is not compromised by the collar

Immobilise lower limbs, use 6 frac straps including a pelvic strap

Log roll on to Orange Basket stretcher / Spine board / PS1

Secure the casualty to the stretcher
If BIG SICK TRANSPORT SLIGHTLY HEAD DOWN

NOTES

Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Spinal injuries - Staying

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, Numbness, Pain, Pins and needles, Incontinence, Loss of movement

DO NOT HOLD HEAD WHILST STANDING

Neil Robertson stretcher or PS1 for helo evacuation

PS1 for ILB evacuation

If BIG SICK and no entonox available GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Consider if you have to move BIG SICK / DANGER
or can you stabilise in location LITTLE SICK

If moving casualty, collar and secure to the stretcher
If BIG SICK TRANSPORT SLIGHTLY HEAD DOWN

NOTES

Be ready to treat unconsciousness SEE CARD 1
Be ready to resuscitate SEE CARD 2

Head injuries - Lying

Possible signs and symptoms:
Breathing rate 30+, Cap refill 3+, unconsciousness, Confusion, Memory loss, Vomiting, Bruising, Bleeding

NEVER GIVE ENTONOX

Reassure, approach from the front, ask not to move

Hold head and neck in neutral alignment

Watch for vomiting and clear airway as required

If BIG SICK GIVE OXYGEN @15 l/min via free flow mask

Dress head wound keeping head still

Consider if you have to move BIG SICK / DANGER
or can you stabilise in location LITTLE SICK

If moving casualty, size and apply collar as long as airway is not compromised by the collar

Immobilise lower limbs, use 6 frac straps including a pelvic strap

Log roll on to Orange Basket stretcher / Spine board / PS1

Secure the casualty to the stretcher, then LOOSEN COLLAR - TRANSPORT SLIGHTLY HEAD UP

NOTES

Be ready to treat unconsciousness using Jaw Thrust SEE CARD 1
